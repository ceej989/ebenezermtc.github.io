A call to lead a life with Transparency

 

             A life with transparency is the need of the hour. Jesus said you are the light of the world and the important quality of light is that it cannot hide anything. Light has another qualities but the most important that I believe is transparency.  The majority of codes of ethics of churches and Christian agencies have the title ‘Code for transparency and against corruption’. Bible gives us ethical and theological reflections about the transparency that every Christian needs to have to lead a life with transparency. Bible says God is light. God came on earth, incarnated in Jesus of Nazareth, to bring light to the darkness. Darkness hinders good relations, it destroys trust, it is an expression of sin, it encourages immoral or illegal practices. ‘The light has come into the world, and people loved darkness rather than light because their deeds were evil. For all who do evil hate the light and do not come to the light, so that their deeds may not be exposed. But those who do what is true come to the light, so that it may be clearly seen that their deeds have been done in God.’ (John 3:19-21) Our God is light and He wants everyone to live in light.

             Secondly God is justice. Equal treatment and just action need transparency. Justice to all is one of the affirmations of the Bible. Old Testament is particular about justice to the poor and warns severe judgment to those who overturns justice. Transparency brings justice to all. Prophet Amos says ‘let justice role like waters and righteousness like an ever flowing stream’. Often we lack justice in our relationships and engagements. God acts justly to everyone and He expects us to keep justice in all our relationships.  

           Thirdly Stewardship and accountability brings transparency in our lives.  Christians are called to be good stewards in the management of goods, services and all gifts. They are lent to us by God, who remains the proprietor. Human beings are responsible to God as his managers and accountants. We are primarily accountable to God, but also to the brothers and sisters of the Christian community, the parish, the church as the body of Christ and therefore the body of the triune God. Mutual transparent accountability is the expression of love, trust, honesty, responsibility and Christian community. In 1 Samuel chapter 12, Samuel is giving his farewell sermon and he is asking these questions: Whose Ox have I taken? Or whose donkey have I taken? Whom Have I defrauded? Whom have I oppressed? Or From whose hand have I taken a bribe to blind my eyes with it? The Pulpit commentary decoded these questions to 1, appropriating wrongfully that belongs to others 2, defrauding them of what is their due 3, oppressing the poor and the weak 4, perverting the proper course of justice for the sake of gift or bribe.

       This challenge is every one of us to lead a life with transparency. A life with transparency is a witness to the life of Jesus who lived as light to the entire world.  Let our personal life, church life, community be an example of transparency and ultimately bring glory to God’s name. I and my family wish everyone a happy and meaningful summer

 

V. M. Mathew achen
