BAGGAGE A BURDEN

Dearly Beloved in Christ,
We are entering Advent season, known as the Kingdom Season. We can see the shops already have their Christmas decorations out, for the people of God. It is a time of reflection and asking ourselves, what does God want his people to look like in our age. Canterbury Arch Bishop Justin Welb says Christmas has not only becomes a celebration of consumerism rather than the Savior’s birth, it has become a hotchpotch of expectation that the reality can’t match. Expensive Christmas can spoil Life. Be generous in a way that shows love and affection. You can’t buy it, you can show it and when you show it, it comes back at you with interest. Share love and affection with reasonable gifts that demonstrate you really care for someone. That makes for the best Christmas you could ever have.
We come round to Christmas every year, He did it once. We go through life accumulating baggage, God is one who brings extraordinary fresh starts, baggage free journeys with Him through life. Travelling light is necessity for the very poor, the asylum seeks and refugee and a luxury for the very wealthy. They have tons of luggages. New starts are wonderful, God designed new start’s are perfect. But so few of us feel that we are in the place where God does that for us, for me the baggage weighs us down, baggage of memories and fears or baggage of wealth and worry. Whether it is luxury or less arrogance or agony, how does the light break on me? How do I become one of that numbers with joy and celebration that is the light of Christ, of the baby in the manger?
The news of the devastating storm in the Philippines is tragic. We are deeply shocked and saddened to hear the loss of thousands of lives and of the sufferings of millions caused by the storm Typhoon Haiyan. We must pray for those who have lost loved ones and all those who are traumatized by the disaster and in desperate need of food, water, shelter and medical attention. We pray for those who are most vulnerable in this crisis; children separated from their parents the sick and injured the disabled and the elderly. May we dare to hope that through the generosity of the privileged, the destitute might glimpse hope, warmth and life again. May the victims of this terrible storm know God’s comfort and derive strength from their faith. Last year we had also faced the situations like Hurricane Sandy .This year it happened in Philippians. So we pray for the people and our responsibility is that help them with open mind and generous hand. MAY GOD BLESS US ALL.
WISH YOU A HAPPY AND PROSPEROUS NEW YEAR AND BLESSED HAPPY CHRISTMAS.
SUJAN ACHEN
