                                                                       LOOKING BEYOND THE SURFACE

DEARLY BELOED IN CHRIST,         

               Each one of us can look at our own personal external history and see what we have achieved. How have I grown as a human being and as a child of god? We have to measure this on virtues we have built up or the inner value systems we have developed. How have we grown in God’s grace? What is the purpose of my life and what am I living for? How is my honesty quotient?    Am I an honest,   sincere, integral human being? Am I just in all that I do and say? Am I a hope- filled, positive person? Am I more loving than what I was a few years ago? If I were to draw a love chart of my life how will it appear?   Am I selfish? Am I self centered? How much of what I learn I generously and secretly give to the poor and for the development of others? Am I socially conscious and politically involved? Is anger, hatred, envy or jealousy eating up my heart? How am I behaving as parent? How do I forgive? Am I a peace loving person? What about my commitment level to my family or my country? How compassionate and caring I as a parent or a boss or coworker? Do I gossip? Am I grateful for all the blessings I have received or am I cribbing and complaining all the time? These are just a few internal growth questions. Only I will truly know who I am within but people will notice the effects of this personal   inner growth.    

            Very often we neglect looking at our internal history, our internal growth chart. In Christian parlance we call it examination of conscience. It can be also seen as our creative, spiritual inner journey. It is important to examine this inner journey and have a clear vision of our inner life. This inner journey or growth is ultimately responsible for our happiness and inner peace. We can also call it the light of our soul. This inner growth or interior strength can help us face any kind of hardship in life. As they say, money can’t buy us happiness.

             Sant Rajinder Singh once narrated the story of a cave that lived underground. This cave spent its entire life in darkness; it had never seen any light. One day, a voice talked to the cave, and said “Come up in to the light; come experience and enjoy the sunlight.” The cave replied, “I do not know what you mean by light. All I have ever known is darkness.” “Come and see for yourself,” said the voice. The cave mustered up enough courage and climbed slowly up from the depths of the earth. Suddenly, it reached the top of the earth and was surrounded by magnificent light, the likes of which the cave had never seen in its life. “This is beautiful,” said the cave. After enjoying the light for a while, the cave said to the sunlight, “Now it is your turn to come with me and see the darkness.” “What is darkness?” asked the sunlight. The cave answered, “Come and see for yourself” So the sunlight decided to visit the cave’s abode. As the sunlight entered the cave, it said, “Now show me your darkness.” But with the sunlight there, there was no darkness to be found! Where there is light, there can be no darkness. Our inner light, the light of the soul dispels the darkness of our external life and give us peace. Our inner nature is our true nature. Our inner nature is our true nature. The outer accomplishments are finite but the inner accomplishments extend in to the eternal. We need to spend more time with our inner growth through silence, meditation and prayer. It is hard work and very rarely seen by others. This growth is not for the sake of getting praise and affirmation but for personal fulfillment and inner peace. Ultimately God will not ask me whether I was successful but whether I was a good human being.

             The following months as church we are celebrating our organizations special services like Voluntary Evangelists Association, Youth Sunday, Family Sunday, Day of people with special skills and also our Annual Convention. Let us pray for the success of these services.

                                                      May God Bless Us All.                                 By,   Sujan Achen
