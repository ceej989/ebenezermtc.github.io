                   CHOOSE GOOD

Dearly beloved in Christ,

Dr. Martin Luther King said, “Every one has the power of greatness not too fame, but greatness, because greatness is determined by service”. Jesus said to his disciples, “A good tree does not bear rotten fruit, nor does a rotten tree bear good fruit.  A good person out of the store of goodness in his heart produce good, but an evil person out of a store of evil produces evil; for from the  fullness of the heart the mouth speaks”(Luke: 6: 43-44).  Jesus likens goodness to a store within.  This store has to be built up patiently.  The foundation is laid by our parents and other adults (teachers, guides, and coaches etc.,) who act as mentors and educators.  Friends also can inspire us.  But this store has to be constantly replenished.  At times we have to take stock of our goodness.  We need silence to reflect whether our thoughts, our words, and our actions are pointing to the goodness within us.  In older days we were all taught to “think well of all, speak well of all, and do good to all”.  This is   the foundation of goodness.  We need to observe whether the live within us, is blocked or whether we are blind to the fact that we are not building on our goodness store.  These wellbeing articles constantly speak of choices.  Our life is all about choices.  We can choose good or evil.  The normal human being is capable of many deeds of goodness.  There are exceptions namely, people who have been brain washed to believe that one has to kill in the name of a particular religion (or others forms of terrorism).  We are all capable of choosing goodness.  But the degree varies in keeping with our formation, and attitudes we have acquired along the way.  But through silence and prayer, we can improve the store of goodness within us.

Patrick maruthummotil died in an accident while returning from his mission trip.  He lived only 26 years.  He walked the extra mile.  He worked on the store of goodness with in him.  He was a good young person living an ordinary life but he made a courageous choice (to leave the comfort and security) and to serve God during his short span of life. Patrick was a young man who embodied Christ in everything that he did, and in the way he served others and the church.  His genuine smile and sincere love towards every one he met, has impacted our church community in a way that left an imprint of Christ on our hearts.  Goodness slowly develops in a habit.  Goodness as a virtue comes easily when we are surrounded by our friends and family.  But Jesus asks us to go the extra mile when it is a question of goodness.  He asks us to be good to the stranger, the poor, the widows and orphans. The story of the Good Samaritan that Jesus narrated is a great example of goodness- chapter six of St. Luke, speaks of goodness. 

The world is filled with stories of goodness and there is no limit to goodness.  The following short stories are just examples of little acts of goodness.  Raisin bread was always at the top of Ruth’s grocery list because that’s what her husband ate for breakfast every morning. Toasted with butter, it sent a warm, cinnamon smell throughout the house.  This elderly couple (Ruth and her husband) lived without a car in their neighborhood in Buffalo, and it became difficult for them to do their shopping.  The children were too far away to help, so they took a taxi.  One day Ruth went to store-after-store in search of raisin bread.  They were with a cab driver who had shuttled them around before.  He wouldn’t let them give up their search.  Finally they headed home with no luck.  The cab driver helped them with their groceries, and Ruth was generous with his tip.  Later that afternoon, the doorbell rang.  Ruth opened the door.  There stood the cab driver holding two loaves of raisin bread. “Your tip was so big”, he said, “It was burning a hole in my pocket. Enjoy!” Raisin bread was in short supply in Ruth’s neighborhood, but neighborliness and goodness sure wasn’t.  We can fill our life with such acts of goodness. St.Paul says, “Put on as God’s chosen ones, holy and beloved, heartfelt compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience, bearing with one another and forgiving one another, if one has a grievance against another; as the Lord has forgiven you, so must you also do”.  This is in fact a goodness list.  May God help us see the opportunities that are always around us to do good!  

                                                                                                 May God Bless Us All

                                                                                                            SUJAN ACHEN
