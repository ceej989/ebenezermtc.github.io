            Live a Value Driven Life                

Dearly Beloved In Christ,
We are moving towards a new year and we are saying good-bye to an old year. It is time to take stock of our life and observe in silence the values that are driving our life. What values do I stand for? Do I listen to that small inner voice that tells me “this is right and this is wrong?” The New Year is a good time to clarify values. It is good to reflect every now and then on my personal vision statement. Do I know my core values? Can I make a list of values that are driving me? This is a good time to revise my values and my life’s purpose, vision or meaning.
It was a Sunny Saturday afternoon in Chennai. A proud father was taking his two little boys to the circus. He walked up to the man at the ticket counter and said, “How much is it to get in?” The young man replied, ‘Rs.100 for you and Rs.75 if your children are below nine and we let them in free if they are six or younger. How old are they? The man replied, “The lawyer’s three and the doctor is seven, so I guess I owe you Rs175. The man at the ticket counter looked surprised and said, Sir, did you just win the lottery or something? You could have told me that the older one was six; I would’t have know the difference”.

The man replied, “Yes, that may be true, but the kids would have known the difference.” There are two ways of living our life; you can just survive, just exist and ‘live and partly live’, as Elliot would say. But there is another way of living, a purpose driven life, a meaningful life, a passionate life or a value-driven life. Stephen Covey says; Every one chooses one of two roads in life- one is the broad, well travelled road to mediocrity, the other road to greatness and meaning.” My life is based and guided by values. Values are the standards, beliefs and principles that I intend to base my life on. I can live a life of true integrity when I am conscious of the values that drive me. The leads to wellbeing and happiness. Values are like a road map. Values provide the foundation, the structure and direction to my life and they connect my aspirations, my inner values and external actions. They provide the basis for knowing when to say ‘Yes’ and when to say ‘no’. They also help me to distinguish between what is important and what is trivial.
Bill Montgomery once said that one of the most important things in life is to decide what is the most important thing in my life. How many people really do that or take the time to give it any thought? We face a lot of problems in life because we do not know the road we are travelling. We find it difficult to make choices. We have not decided what is important to us and what our personal guiding principles are. There are positive values and negative values. To value something is to esteem it to be of worth to my life and direction my life is going to take. And values are critically important. But we can value many different things; integrity, humility, love, compassion, forgiveness, passion, hard work or money in the bank, status, fame or tons of money, Just because we value money it cannot become a happiness bringing purpose. As Stephen Covey says When what we value is in opposition to the natural laws that govern peace of mind and quality of life, we base our lives on illusion and set ourselves up for failure”

Show me your ways, O Lord, teach me your paths; guide me in your truth and teach me, for you are God my Savior, and my hope is in you all day long”[ psalm 25: 4-5] MAY THIS NEW YEAR BEGIN WITH A CLARIFICATION OF CORE VALUES AND THE GOVERNING PRINCIPLES OF OUR PERSONAL LIFE* HAPPY AND BLESSED NEW YEAR.

By,
Sujan Achen
