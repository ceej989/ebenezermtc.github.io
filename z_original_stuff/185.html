‘Freedom is not free’

 

Recently we visited Washington DC and found this beautiful phrase ‘Freedom is not free’ at the Korean War memorial. It attracted all of our attention and it clearly tells the world something that every one of us has to remember in life. On July 4th we celebrated the independence day of  United States and on August 15th we celebrated the independence day of India.  In all these celebrations if we forget the cost of the freedom that we have today these celebrations are meaningless. Three is a cost to every freedom that we have and someone else have already paid the price for it. We are living in a world that gives a lot of importance to individual freedom and it appreciates the exercise of this freedom to the extent of not hurting anyone.  Some people want to use this freedom to do whatever they want without hurting anyone. In this scenario the common good or the good to the other is not addressed. So, is the freedom that we experience today beneficial to anyone? If not we need to rethink about it and reframe our ideas to reshape our freedom.

Galatians 5. 1 says “It is for freedom that Christ has set us free. Stand firm, then, and do not let yourselves be burdened again by a yoke of slavery.” What is the freedom of Christians who experience salvation? That is the point that Apostle Paul is trying to explain here. As a person who lives in Christ, experiences a freedom and that freedom is a mastery over the evil and sin. Christ’s death on the cross defeated all the yokes of the slavery of sin and brought freedom from sin. Freedom from sin and the experience of salvation is not a freedom to sin again. When we are in Christ we experience a dominion over sin and we are no longer salves to sin. This happens when we have the Grace of God because we are human beings and we are not capable enough to do that by our own. God will give us the grace to have a mastery over sin and that is the freedom in Christ.

But Paul asks another important question in Romans 6. 1- 4  “What shall we say, then? Shall we go on sinning so that grace may increase? 2 By no means! We are those who have died to sin; how can we live in it any longer? 3 Or don’t you know that all of us who were baptized into Christ Jesus were baptized into his death? 4 We were therefore buried with him through baptism into death in order that, just as Christ was raised from the dead through the glory of the Father, we too may live a new life.”

So Christ has set us free to experience a new life of freedom with Him and a freedom from sin. Christ paid the price for this freedom and that was His life. No one will pay their own life to set someone else free. That is the life, death and resurrection of our Lord Jesus. This freedom results in a new life and that life will lead many into this freedom and new life. Let us all experience this freedom to live in Christ and for Christ.

 

V. M. Mathew achen

Youth Chaplain
