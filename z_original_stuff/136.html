DISCIPLINE MAKES THE DIFFERENCE

 

Dearly beloved in Christ,

          Wilma Rudolph was born four months premature, in 1940, in Tennessee a U.S state.   Wilma suffered various ailments throughout her child hood in addition to shorter left leg.  In her fourth year of life, she had pneumonia and scarlet fever, and became wheel chair bound for two years.  During these moments of struggle and hardship, Wilma’s mom sang songs reflecting God’s love for Wilma and how special she will be one day.  Wilma started attending school, when she could walk with leg brace.  Classmates started calling her nick name “Limpy”, which stayed with her throughout schooling.  Wilma loved sports and began to play basketball, negating her disability from impeding sense of optimism and desire to run, jump, and play.  Her optimism and discipline made her become part of both school and state team.  Ed Temple, women’s track coach, saw in her a disciplined winner.  He was impressed with her determination, self-confidence, and hard work.  She won bronze medal in the 1956 Olympics, in the 400 meter relay.  The public criticized the coach, for taking a crippled black girl as one of their representatives.  But Wilma went to Rome.  It was an amazing victory for a crippled black girl from America: She won the Gold medal in the 100 and 200 meters race by defeating outstanding women athletes, and a Gold for the 400 meter relay.

        What was the secret of her success? How Wilma did this?  The first step is self- acceptance leading to the development of a positive self – image.  She accepted her limitations and abilities. Her mother’s love and confidence might have helped her in forming this great self image.  This positive self-image helped her to form her own vision of life, to excel in sports, and bring glory to her family, race and her country.  It is not enough to dream, it is not enough to be positive, it is not enough to have a vision.  We need a plan, focus, and above all self- discipline, and hard work.                                          

       Former Indian President Dr. Abdul Kalam tells young people “dream and sweat if you want to succeed in life”.  Hard work and self discipline without a vision does not lead us to great heights and a vision or a dream without hard work and self discipline will also not lead us anywhere.  In the words of Jesse Owens “but in order to make dreams come in to reality, it takes an awful lot of determination, dedication, self- discipline, and effort”.  Discipline is the watchword of great performers.  Discipline makes the difference between good and the great.  Discipline is the ability to hold on, work hard till we reach our goal.  It could be a value to be lived or attained or a task undertaken.  We could achieve what we want to achieve with patience, hard work, and persistence.  We should not lose focus.  The fulfillment of these goals or promises builds confidence and self – esteem, which eventually leads champions to believe almost anything, is possible.                                                                             A proper life vision and self- discipline leads to wellbeing and happiness.  If you have to succeed in your workplace, you have to learn to discipline yourself.  When we are disciplined, we learn to situate sense of duty before pleasure. Many of us do not progress in our spiritual life, family life or career because of the lack of planning, focus, hard work, and persistence.  This is discipline.  Discipline means I know where I want to go and I will give up everything to reach my destination.  So let us discipline ourselves and make the difference!!!  

                                                                                    May the GOD BLESS US ALL.

                                                                                              Sujan Achen
