                    LIVING A PASSIONATE LIFE

Dearly Beloved in Christ,

                                           A man once visited a temple under construction where he saw sculptor working on an idol of God. Suddenly he noticed a similar idol lying nearby. Surprised, he asked the sculptor, ” Do you need two statues of the same God?”.No, said the sculptor without looking up. “We need only one , but the first one got damaged at the last stage.”The gentleman examined the idol and found no apparent damage ,”where is the damage?”he asked. “There is a scratch on the nose of the idol.” Said the sculptor, still busy with his work. “Where are you going to install the idol?” The sculptor replied that it would be installed on a pillar twenty feet high. If the idol is that far, who is going to know that there is a scratch on the nose? The gentleman asked. The sculptor stopped his work, looked up at the gentleman, smiled and said, “I will know it.” Can we call this a passion for excellence? The desire to excel is exclusive of the fact whether someone else appreciates it or not. Excellence’ and Passion’ are drives from the inside, not outside. Passion means  to suffer or to endure. Passion is an intense emotion compelling feeling, enthusiasm, or desire for something. Today ever more than before this term is often applied to a lively or eager interest in a cause or activity, in something we do with unusual excitement and enthusiasm. It is a strong positive affinity towards the mission entrusted to us. It is good to see famous musicians on stage or great footballers on the field to understand what passion means. Their non verbal language will communicate this passion and it will spread as a vibration onto all the spectators.

           Jesus was passionate about life. He lived his public life for just three years and two thousand years later people still follow him and speak about him with great passion. Jesus spoke about his Kingdom as a boundless passion for life, for love and He gave his life “that we may have life and life in abundance.’ Today there are people living passionate lives because they are inspired by Jesus. Passion removes fear. Passions brings contentment even in the face of difficulty. Passion never says “I cannot”. Passion engulfs a person passion keeps you awake on your bed. Passions throws you against manmade walls. Passion never allows you to get discouraged. Passion pushes you to the limits of endurance. Passion challenges you to greater heights and is never satisfied with the ordinary. Passion makes you give your life for the particular cause of mission and makes you 100% committed.

Every person has a potential to be passionate. Everyone loves something. We are shaped and motivated by what we love. Every person we see around us is the result of a passionate encounter of two people – it is the force that ensures the creation of new life. Creation itself is the result of a generative love. Passion is at the heart of life. Passion always does not mean fire and noise – passion is also active and revealed in silence. At the heart of a passionate mission is not just the external but  the burning desire for something great  and noble and it does not always require external pomp, glory , noise and excitement. At the heart of a passionate life is a mission, an inspiration or a cause or a person I believe in and I am committed to fully. We are called to follow our heart, discover our highest gifts, make life count, make life memorable and transform the ordinary into extra ordinary.  

                                                                                 May The God Bless Us All,

                                                                                                               Sujan Achen
