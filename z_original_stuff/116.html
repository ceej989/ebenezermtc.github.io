AN EXPRESSION OF GOD’S UNCONDITIONAL LOVE

Dearly Beloved in Christ,

All agree that God’s love for humankind is unconditional, because his love for us remains unchanged, whatever we are or we do. Our sins, that is, our refusal to be loved and to remain called to the love of others, do not change God’s loving attitude towards us. As far as He is concerned, we remain His beloved child and friend. The relational warmth in love is mutual and in that sense conditional, but love in itself is not conditional. The more secure a person is, the more he is capable of unconditional love. The whole bible speaks of the unconditional love of God for humankind. Sometimes, we don’t feel His unconditional love because of our wrong doings. So we take a step to ask forgiveness from God through confession. The idea of forgiveness, as observed in human relationships is not applicable to God’s relation to us. Our sins do not affect His love for us. God has nothing to forgive if sin is the object of forgiveness. He continues to shower His love on each and everyone.                                                                                                                                                                       

The sacrament of Reconciliation is a celebration of God’s unconditional love to the sinner. The sinner who has experienced God’s love, kindness and forgiveness comes to the community of believers to confess, to profess, to proclaim that he has been loved by God, even in his refusal to remain in God’s love. When the sinner confesses his sins to the community, the community rejoices and celebrates the unconditional love of God for him. This celebration is necessary, through it the sinful church builds itself up. Every day at the beginning of the Eucharistic celebration we ask God’s unconditional love. What does it mean when we ask God to forgive our sins? We are expressing our desire and decision not to stay away from His love and to express the unconditional love of Him. When we say that we are sorry for our sins, we are acknowledging that we had closed ourselves to God’s love, we take full responsibility for our action and we affirm our desire to love God more than in the past.             

In Mat;6;14-15,”For if you forgive others the wrongs they have done, your Heavenly Father will also forgive you; but you do not forgive others then the wrongs you have done will not be forgiven by your Father.’’ When we forgive a brother, a fellow human being shows that we are experiencing God’s love within. If one does not forgive his brother, his heart remains closed and is unable to experience the love of god and remain in a state of opposition to himself. So only in forgiving him opens his heart and allows the love of god to flow within him. So the importance of realization is that each one of us is an instrument and channel of salvation for others.

                                         MAY GOD BLESS YOU                                BY, SUJAN ACHEN
