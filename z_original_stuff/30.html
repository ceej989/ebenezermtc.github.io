Vicars Message

Greetings to you all in the precious name of our lord and savior Jesus Christ.

During this month Mar Thoma Church is celebrating Voluntary Evangelist’s Day (5th), Youth Sunday (19th), and Christian Family Sunday (26th)

Also the Regional Convention will be held from October 23rd-26th

Prof David Abraham ,well known convention speaker, will be giving the message. Since Edavaka Mission is giving leadership to the convention, its members have to take active participation in the arrangement for the well functioning of the convention.

The motto of Voluntary Evangelistic association is ‘Each Marthomite is an                    Evangelist’. Evangelistic Association is a lay people’s movement. Let us think about the Antiochian Church. The Church in Antioch did not have an Apostle working in its founding. The Church in Antioch was born completely from the work of lay people. We don’t even know their names, only where they were from. The work was done because believers were willing to speak their faith. They had simple and personal conversation with people about what they believed. These believers simply went into Antioch and shared with those they met what Jesus had done in their lives. They explained the reason for their faith. These believers shared the truth about Jesus and the good news that the forgiveness of sins and the promise of salvation was available for all. If the church is going to expand and grow again, believers must be involved.

Secondly, they were impartial and to share the truth with everyone – Those were multiracial, multi-cultural believers from the island of Cyprus and Cyrene, a place in northern Africa. They were more open and willing to share the truth with everyone. . They shared the good news of Jesus with anyone and everyone that they came into contact with. Thirdly the power of God was with them and upon their work. We need the Hand of Christ upon us and all that we do.  The hand of Jesus is powerful. The hand of Jesus still transforms lives. The hand of Jesus gives strength to the weak. The hand of Jesus gives courage to the fearful. The hand of Jesus gives hope to the hopeless. When the laity gets involved and shares their stories, the good news of Jesus, His hand will be with them and transformation will take place. We are called to go into the Antioch of life and get involved. Get Involved, Share the Good News and watch the Lord work.

May God bless us all.

Johnson Cherupuzhethu Atchen
