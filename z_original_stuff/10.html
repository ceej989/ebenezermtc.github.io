Mar Thoma youth Fellowship, North East Region

Jubilee night

 

Dear beloved in Christ

 

This is to inform you that the North East regional Youth fellowship is celebrating the 25th Jubilee of our Diocese through a Jubilee night which will be held on 5 pm on Sunday 28th July 2013 at the Bayside High School auditorium. Our young members will be presenting their talents through Praise and worship, Instrumental concert, Dance fusion and a Drama. Our Diocesan Bishop Rt. Rev. Geevarghese Mar Theodosius Episcopa will give the message. The admission to this program will be through donation tickets of $250, $200 and $100 which accommodate a family of four members. The individual donation ticket will be of $30. All the proceeds from this event will be used for the paying off of the Youth chaplains’ residence’s mortgage and that will eventually reduce the share of each parish that supports the Youth Chaplains’ ministry.   We request the following things from the parish members

1.      We request your prayerful participation in this program

2.      All the young members who are willing to participate in Praise and worship, instrumental concert, Dance fusion and Drama are requested to contact the Youth Chaplain Rev. V. M. Mathew or Sherin Daniel the regional secretary.

3.      We request all the families to buy tickets from the youth fellowship office bearers to support this event.

 

We look forward to your support and encouragement.

 

Yours in His ministry

 

Rev. V. M. Mathew (Youth Chaplain)

Binu Daniel (Vice President)

Sherin Daniel (Secretary)

Liju Koshy (Treasurer)





Marketing Christ or Marking Christ

“Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you. And surely I am with you always, to the very end of the age.” (Mat. 28. 19, 20)

Jesus’ commandment before leaving this world, after the resurrection, is very crucial to the mission of the church today. What is the commandment of Jesus here? Jesus’ commandment is to make disciples of all the nations. Making disciples is not an easy task and Jesus clearly states the ways to do that. Make disciples is the main verb of verses 19 and 20, so Jesus’ mandate here is to make disciples of all the nations. Some people misinterpreted this as a commandment for baptism and some others misunderstood this as a commandment only to preach the Gospel. Through the years this commandment is being distorted by reducing the mission as to market Christ. Christ is becoming a product that s being sold out in a competitive Market. The number of sales representatives is increasing and they are using all the modern techniques and apps to market Christ in whatever way possible. All the modern communication methods are being used to communicate about Christ. New apps, new music, new webs and new channels are used to preach Christ but after so many centuries how many disciples are there who followed and following ‘the way’ that Jesus wanted us to obey?  I am not trying to criticize anyone but trying to make a self-criticism asking to myself am I marketing Christ or Marking Christ. This is the biggest challenge before every Christian especially to those who are in the ministry.

Here we have to look at the correct meaning of this commandment of Jesus. This commandment is all about witnessing Jesus and I would call it as marking Christ. Our words and our deeds have to mark Christ. Jesus clearly states that obeying what he has commanded is the way to become a disciple of Christ. Where are our Christ markings today? We are called to live out according to the teachings of Christ. We spend a lot of money and energy in marketing Christ but we are challenged to carry the marking of Christ in our life. Today The Gospel is calling us to be witnesses of Christ Jesus by living a life worthy of His teachings. We have many models in front of us who marked Christ in their lives. The apostles, people like mother Theresa, Father Damien, Martin Luther King, and William Carrey are few examples who marked Christ in their lives and they are all still living as true witnesses of Jesus. Let us dedicate us to mark Christ in our lives.

V. M. Mathew achen
