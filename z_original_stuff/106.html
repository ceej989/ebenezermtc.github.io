Vicar’s Message     Perseverance Brings Success

                        If there is one word that can sum up all that can stand for the foundation of that experience we call it success –it is perseverance. Perseverance leads to success and success with value or with greatness can lead to wellbeing.  This is another everyday choice we have to make it our life. When we fail or fall we have the choice to remain where we fell or failed or to get up and start working /walking again. We have to make the choice of being at it day after day in order to be successful and happy. Perseverance can be defined as not giving up, it is constancy in effort, trying again and again, overcoming obstacles with humility, firmness, patience, constancy, hard work and prayer. It is the never-say-die attitude.

                         The American Heritage dictionary defines perseverance as “steady persistence in adhering to a course of action, a belief, or a purpose; steadfastness.” It defines endurance as “the act, quality, or power of withstanding hardship or stress,” but in the second definition it defines endurance as “the state or act of persevering.” While they are synonyms and each word carries in it the idea of “continuance,” perseverance lays stress on a given course of action in the face of difficulty or opposition.

History places before us two kinds of people, the ones who fought the good fight till the end and the ones who gave up at the first obstacle.Some persons like – Thomas Edison-who invented the light bulb,Walt Disney – whose dream was to create “Happiest Place on Earth”, Colonel Sanders – “Kentucky Fried Chicken” and J.K. Rowling -the author of the Harry Potter books are some examples of perseverance.

 All these great heroes teach us one lesson – never give up, persevere, endure and work hard. Perseverance is needed for growth in spiritual life, to succeed in your work place, to be a successful person. As a parent you need perseverance and endurance day after day. All good goals need these. This is a choice we have to make. Jesus once said,” If you put your hand on the plough, then do not look back.” The important thing in life is not the triumph but to live well. It is a struggle. The essential thing is……..to have fought well. It reminds us of St. Paul’s last words to encourage Timothy:” I have fought the good fight, I have finished the course, and have kept the faith.”

God Bless Us All

In His service- SujanAchen
