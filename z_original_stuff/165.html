A servant Leader

 

Isa 42:1-7 is the first of four "Servant Songs" in Isaiah, referring to God's Servant, the Messiah. The leadership model that we receive in this servant song is a unique one. This is pointing to the messianic arrival and the way of His functioning but definitely a leadership model that Christian leader should follow. Isaiah is presenting this leader entirely different from the leadership models that we see in the Christian leadership today. Today many perceive and follow a leadership that is attached to the brutal greed of power, money and favouritism. So we experience a considerable reduction of leadership models that is to be looked up or to be followed. Isaiah is clearly presenting to us the best leadership model in this servant song. A leader must be a servant and that is the clear model of the leadership that we receive from this passage. This servant image is something that we all are lacking in our leadership in the church and society. Jesus literally practiced this model when he was in His ministry and confirmed this prophesy of a servant leadership. Where do we see this servant image of leadership today? I believe that is the greatest virtue a leader should follow as a Christian leader. This passage clearly says that God will put his spirit upon this servant leader. This is the promise that the bible gives to the servant leaders; they are not by their spirits but the spirit f the Lord will be their strength. What are the functions of a servant leader? This passage the servant leader will be the one that uphold justice and righteousness. That will be the basic guideline for the functioning of this leader; he or she will be a carrier of Justice and righteousness. Verse 2 is talking about another aspect of the leadership.  “He will not shout or cry out, or raise his voice in the streets.” This gives a different perspective on leadership without any pomp and noises. Many a time the leaders are becoming like people who lives in name and fame, looking only to live with clamour and shows. But a servant leader leads with humility and meekness.

Verse 3 clearly portrays the focus of this leader. The purpose of this servant leadership is to focus on what is fragile and vulnerable, what is weak and unsupported, what is dying and experiencing inequality. We, as leaders, are mostly guided by the powerful forces and we normally stand with them. In his recent apostolic exhortation called Evangelii Gaudium, Pope Francis wrote: “as long as the problems of the poor are not radically resolved by rejecting the absolute autonomy of markets and financial speculation and by attacking the structural causes of inequality, no solution will be found for the world’s problems or, for that matter, to any problems” The servant leader will focus his/her leadership in resolving the problem of the poor, weak and fragile. Verse 4 talks about the mission that is entrusted to a leader and that has to continue until the justice is to be established. Many of us are complacent and lethargic in our mission and ministry. We are satisfied with the status quo and want to be stereotyped always. But this passage says that the servant leader will be in the field until the justice is established.

This passage is challenging every Christian to follow the paths of servant leadership. God expects us to be servant leaders in the various leadership roles that are entrusted to us. May God bless us all.

 

V. M. Mathew achen

Youth Chaplain
