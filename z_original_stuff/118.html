                          Nothing is Impossible               

   St. Mathew’s Christmas story about the birth of Jesus our Savior, portrays the long-awaited Messiah as the fulfillment of the hopes and desires of all human kind. “All this took place to fulfill what had been spoken by the Lord through the prophet.”(Mt:1:22).Occurring almost like a refrain in the Infancy Narrative, these words of Mathew have most often been applied solely to the birth of Jesus, as if only the birth of Messiah is the fulfillment of the encouraging message of liberation from all bonds pronounced by the prophets! Jesus as Savior come to reveal the daring plan of God, an unveiled the great heights to which human life could rise when united in covenant with God. This revelation of Jesus, however, is not altogether new. Several Old Testament events give us glimpses of what God’s infinite power can achieve when shared with human beings.

                  Beginning with Abraham, the father of all believers, we see that the toughest challenge for him was to believe in “Limitless Possibilities”. God promised him a large progeny yet he knew only too painfully that he and his wife Sarah were beyond the age of child-bearing. He had to disregard all evidence to the contrary and continue to live with Sarah as husband and wife which he faithfully did. Eventually, his faith was rewarded when  “the Lord dealt with Sarah as he had said……and Sarah conceived and bore a son.” What was definitely a dead end, turned out to be a bright new beginning for Abraham and for all believers! Once we believe that the possibilities are limitless, miracles begin to occur all around us. The airplane came into being simply because the Wright Brothers refused to believe that a heavy object cannot fly through the air. Almost all discoveries and inventions up to now have occurred when someone dared to break out of a restricting routine. Personal growth too is evident only when an established paradigm of behavior shifts because of some insurmountable obstacle, forcing us to see things in a new way!

                The birth of Jesus Christ then proclaims anew this fundamental truth that ‘nothing is impossible for God.’ Is it surprising that we find these words on the lips of Gabriel precisely as he announces the birth of Jesus? Being a virgin, Mary whom Gabriel addressed also found herself in an apparently impossible situation! How could she, a virgin, be a mother? Yet because she stepped out of her limitations in faith, we today have access to a higher level of living in which God and human beings are literally one!  To celebrate Christmas, then, should mean that we live by the firm conviction that today too, all things are possible for God and us. Abraham, Mary and the rest are not rare exceptions, but rather pointers that Life itself, as a rule, is meant to be “open-ended”. Breaking out of the cocoon of our comfort zone and heeding the call of Life, we need to step out beyond it in faith and scale the heights. Christmas reminds us that the ball is in our court-can we believe and continue the work of creation?

         “ WISH  YOU  ALL  A  HAPPY  MERRY  X’MAS  AND  A  PROSPEROUS  NEW  YEAR.”

                                                                                                                     SUJAN ACHEN
